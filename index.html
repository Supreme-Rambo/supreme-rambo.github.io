This blog serves as a platform to share ongoing research and insights into the capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs) when tasked with converting raw Indicators of Compromise (IOCs) into structured STIX (Structured Threat Information eXpression) formats. About This Research

In the evolving landscape of cybersecurity, the timely and accurate exchange of threat intelligence is paramount. STIX (Structured Threat Information eXpression) provides a standardized language for this, but manually converting diverse IOCs (e.g., IP addresses, file hashes, URLs, domain names) into STIX can be time-consuming and error-prone. Our research investigates the efficacy of LLMs in automating this conversion process.

Specifically, we aim to:

  • Quantify the accuracy of various LLMs in identifying and extracting different types of IOCs from unstructured text.
  • Evaluate their performance in mapping these extracted IOCs to appropriate STIX objects and properties.
  • Identify common failure modes and biases in LLM-based STIX conversion.
  • Propose methodologies for improving LLM accuracy and reliability in this domain.
